SEO for Ecommerce Notes

  • Introduction
  • SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization
  • We’ll talk about the importance of usability on your site and the difference between on and off-page SEO
  • Then research customers and how to create buyer personas to guide and unify your marketing efforts
  • Review common mistakes made on ecommerce websites
  • We’ll go over shopify and Adobe Muse exported as HTML files
  • Platforms to build sites makes it simple to change SEO data and information
  • Google Analytics, a free website-tracking and analytics suite provided by Goolge
  • Understanding Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
  • SEO is the process of assessing the current on and off page factors of a website and improving these factors to boost rankings in a search engine like Google
  • There is a big connection to a site’s traffic and it’s search engine
  • You have to build traffic to build rankings
  • Google’s job is to give the most relevant and useful results it can for a search term
  • We can directly control on-page factors often by simply changing them right in the Ecommerce provider’s interface or within the code
  • Off-page factors, Changes or tactics that you can use to boost rankings that don’t involve modifying the pages of your site
  • On-page factors can only be optimized to a certain point, you can only tweak your site so much
  • The most important rule of SEO is to ensure that your site is easy to use for customers and potential buyers first
  • Acquisition and retention are basically the same thing now, they’re both important for SEO
  • Always design and write for customers and visitors first and search engines second
  • SEO is typically implemented deeper in the buying process
  • Shifting your SEO higher into the wider part of the funnel is good for potential users
  • Rankings can adjust up and down on a daily or weekly basis
  • SEO requires constant measurement and monitoring
  • Targeting a niche market is better than going up against big companies
  • You gotta fake it til you make it to get to the top of a search engine
  • Researching Your Customers
  • You have to find your key demographic for your selling point
  • Key in on exactly on your customers so you can move up on the search engine
  • Target personas that most likely to make a purchase on your site, not just visit
  • The goal to create buyer persona is to help better tailor your message and gives you a reference point to test your strategies against
  • Get into the shoes of your customers to figure what they want
  • Buyer personas are an extremely helpful tools for making sure your SEO efforts are on target and for correctly bringing in the right kind of traffic
  • Keyword research is the most important aspect of Ecommerce SEO strategy
  • Searches with fewer words generally get a much higher volume
  • Simple keywords fall at the beginning where the search volume is highest and long-tail keywords at the end, where there’s less volume and it’s much more specific
  • Sit down and simply write out an initial list of keywords that a buyer may enter
  • Keyword brainstorming and targeting is the foundation for optimizing your ecommerce site, so take your time and build a high quality list
  • You have to analyze your competitors and why they are ranking on the top
  • Identify the key players then use a tool called SEMrush to analyze other top ranking sites for particular keywords
  • Title and meta description are very important for SEO efforts so make a note of them
  • Differentiate yourself as much as possible and capitalize on a smaller segment of the market
  • Auditing for Site Usability and Structure
  • The page title is the most important factor to improve SEO
  • You must use a unique page title for each page title for each page of your website, google may penalize you if it detects duplicate titles throughout your site
  • Avoid stuffing your page full of keywords if you can
  • Be sure to use a unique description for each page on your website
  • If you’re using a design application , you often have an read within the app that you can input this SEO information
  • It’s important to write both title and descriptions for maximum comprehension
  • Structuring your page helps a search engine better understand the context of your website
  • You page heading is often the first element that a user sees on the page, so make sure it’s easy to understand
  • Make sure the product description uses any important keywords that you’re looking to target
  • Make sure to include a small description of all of the images within our site for SEO
  • If your site doesn’t have headings, body copy, or alt tags then that’s good reason why your site isn’t coming on search engines
  • It’s very important that your site contains links so that pages aren’t lost or buried where search spiders can’t find them
  • Any link created that points to another web page contains something called “anchor text”
  • The easier you make it for users to explore deeper, the better chance you have of them staying on the site for longer
  • Make sure that there is a clear and logical path for both users and search spider to follow to find all of the pages on your site. If your page isn’t seen, then it won't be ranked
  • Building links from external websites, or rather, earning those links, is crucial for improving result for a particular keyword
  • External links are even more powerful if the anchor text is optimized and the content on both the source and target pages are relevant
  • Building a better quality inbound links rather than trying to build thousand of inbound links from low-ranking or spammy websites is very good for SEO
  • Utilizing SEO Best Practices
  • Duplicate content is a sin in the world of SEO because it confuses search engines
  • Always write clear and unique page titles and descriptions for each product page on your site, never reuse them.
  • We need to implement a canonical tag telling Google which page is the original
  • The Concept of keyword density in SEO refers to the total number of targeted keywords on the page divided by the total number of words on the page
  • Cloaking is very bad for SEO
  • Avoid unnatural keyword usage
  • Make sure to avoid any slow loading speeds caused by the use of rich media
  • Ensure that large images or videos don’t negatively impact your website load speed
  • When uploading videos on your site, always make sure to provide a summary or some content about the video nearby on the web page
  • Always make sure that you’re hosting your videos somewhere that works on an ios device
  • Slow loading or buggy media players can frustrate your visitors and discourage them from exploring deeper into your website
  • Use PageSpeed Insights to gain some insights on how your web page is doing
  • Make sure you’re using a quality host
  • If you’re self-hosting your website make sure you’re using somebody reputable
  • Minify your code to increase load speeds
  • Recommended that you don’t self-host the files, use a CDN that can distribute them from a geographically diverse data centers to improve load speed
  • You can use your goal and conversion tracking to measure the overall increase in sales on your website
  • Using Google Analytics, setup goals to analyze your web page
  • Implementing On-Page Optimization
  • Try to find a domain name that contains a keyword related to their industry.
  • If you exist in a more crowded space, you may want to consider a unique word that you’ve made up, or combined with another word, that closely relates to your industry or market
  • Avoid uncommon top-level domains like .cc, .info, .biz,
  • Avoid spammy sounding domain names with hyphens
  • Homepages should be full of calls to action
  • Make it immediately clear what you do or sell and do so in a prominent, heading-friendly way
  • Focus on quickly informing users of the product benefits in a streamlined way.
  • Your number one goal here is to encourage visitors to come into the store and to click deeper into the site to explore products
  • The product page should only educate visitors about the specific details of the product
  • A breadcrumb is a link that shows a user where they are on the site, it’s extremely helpful within e commerce sites as a way for users to navigate back to category pages they were browsing in
  • Reinforce your product page to ensure that your product descriptions, which is the block of text that describes the product, is unique and crafted for each product
  • Check for friendly-mobile badge
  • Use the mobile-friendly testing tool to check
  • Make sure your web page is setup to display correctly on a mobile
  • Use responsive design to have the browser scale accordingly to each platform
  • be even more careful to reduce the length of titles, headings, and content to better fit the smaller screen size
  • Incorporating Measurement and Optimization Tools
  • A-B-Testing is also called Split Test and it means that you're going to show slightly different versions of your site to a segment of your traffic.
  • A preferred tool for split testing uses a service called Optimizely
  • Using optimizely loads a preview of your website, you’ll be able to actually click on elements to edit them
  • Make variations and compare them to the original to optimized your goals and target audience
  • Testing seems initially, complex but it's a common misconception that you need to actually create alternate pages directly on your site
  • Make sure to use Google Analytics to better understand the demographic of your traffic
  • There are many powerful tools on Google Analytics, such as acquisition info
  • Google Analytics is a large application and there are many powerful features you may find beneficial to your SEO tracking efforts
  • A heatmapping tool provide a color coded guide to the specific areas that are getting user attention
  • A Snapshot is like an experiment that we've run and it allows us to measure and monitor what users are doing on specific pages of the site
  • The scroll map is designed to show you how far down a page the users are scrolling
  • Tracking scripts are used to view report and generate heatmaps and scroll maps immediately
  • Implementing design tweaks that encourage users to stay on the site can have a major impact on your rankings
  • Moz is a suite of SEO products designed for larger-scale sites from analytics to page gradings, this tool contains everything you need to quickly identify trouble spots in your SEO efforts
  • Moz helps you understand your current rankings
  • Moz generates a report to show all sorts of information about the site
  • Moz is extremely powerful tool for monitoring your SEO efforts, but it comes with a price tag, but it’s worth the costs
