WordPress eCommerce Core Concepts

What is NPM? / Optimizing images for the Web / SEO for eCommerce / UI/UX Designer / WordPress eCommerce Core Concepts

  • Wordpress Ecommerce
  • Introduction
  • Define your audience and create a website
  • This course is all about adding ecommerce functionality to a WordPress site
  • Understanding Your Market
  • Choose a product to sell
  • Think about your business model first, then the specific software you need
  • Pick an area of interest
  • Explore the different ways you can sell products, physical or virtual
  • Learn the many ways to create or acquire the physical product
  • If virtual figure out how to create that
  • Define your audience
  • Create personas to figure out your audience
  • So make up a character and see how they would view your product
  • Add details to each persona to tailor down to what a person would want
  • If you set your heart on a specific WordPress theme before thinking about your audience, you may pick something that looks pretty, but doesn't resonate with your audience
  • Organize your products
  • Like at pricing model and figure out what is better, free or cost
  • You can also sell products as add-ons to your main product
  • If you sell highly configurable products like computers, it's probably worth your time to make sure your ecommerce platform can handle configurable products
  • Subscriptions are also available to make it easier for your customers to acquire your products
  • Offer pre-sale to validate your product
  • Try selling one product before developing an entire product line
  • Make sure that people really want your product and not just interested in your product
  • Crowdfunding can also validate people’s interest in your product
  • You can also get feedback from potential customers
  • Marketing Your Site
  • Use marketing to increase site traffic
  • The best way to have people visit your store is to use Ads
  • The best to use are Google AdWords and Facebook Advertising
  • Another option is to use Search Engine Optimization to get people to your site
  • Content marketing is very slow and takes a long time to see a payoff
  • Social Media will also get people to your site
  • Understand your customer lifetime value (CLV)
  • You need to lower your acquisition cost against your CLV
  • CLV is the amount of money you’ll make from a customer’s lifetime
  • Increase CLV with repeat customers
  • Figure out how to get your customers coming back to you for repeat business
  • Use social media, newsletters, and marketing automation to show your customers that you still exist
  • Don’t blast your customers with ads nonstop
  • Remind your customers brand by sending them an email on their birthday or if they haven’t made a purchase in a while or entice to come back with a coupon
  • Increase your average order value
  • Offer a premium version of your product to attract new customers or encourage existing customers to increase their average order value
  • Price anchoring will make your usual products look cheaper in comparison to your premium products to entice to buy thinking it’s cheaper
  • Cross-sells are when you promote a related item, hoping the user will buy both
  • Selling Products
  • To get paid online you would need a website, a payment gateway, a merchant account, and a bank account for your business
  • The payment gateway will regulate the customers funds
  • The gateway itself verifies the funds and draws them in the modern gateway
  • Examine your needs
  • What type of payment would you like to accept
  • Some customers prefer paypal over credit cards
  • If a gateway doesn’t support your currency except exchange fees
  • Figure out if you’re selling products one at a time or do you need recurring payments
  • If you only have a few products, you may not need a fully featured shopping cart, and an easy to set up solution like Gumroad may be a better use of your time
  • Checking out with Paypal
  • There are many ways to use paypal, like paypal express and paypal advanced
  • Once you've paid, PayPal will redirect you back to the store, and the store receives a notification from PayPal that payment has been received
  • Checking out with Stripe
  • Stripe is one of the many payment gateways that can allow customers to pay with a credit card directly in your checkout
  • Any time you are accepting credit cards on your site, make sure you have an SSL Certificate
  • Shipping, Orders,and Taxes
  • Calculate shipping costs
  • There’s many ways to ship your products
  • Flat rate shipping, usually a per order or a per item cost
  • You can offer free shipping a certain price point, customers will spend a bit more to avoid shipping charges
  • Manage your orders
  • Wordpress provides a few roles for managing your site that limit Users to writing content, or just being able log in
  • Using stripe or woocommerce, you can be able to refund a partial or total amount
  • It’s a good idea to investigate how the refund process works on your platform
  • Manage your inventory and listings across platforms
  • Putting products on both big sites like amazon and your own website is best for both worlds
  • You have to manage the inventory and product data
  • There are many services that you have choose from to find the perfect solution for your website
  • You have to understand the taxes
  • Tax can come from State, City, Country
  • You usually collect one set of taxes
  • In Europe, they display tax-inclusive prices
  • In the US, they display tax-exclusive prices
  • Some states in the U.S. make certain types of food, clothing, medicine, or medical equipment tax exempt.
  • You'll have to consult with your tax professional to determine if any of your products meet that criteria. Before you launch your site, you should reach out to a tax professional
  • Collec EU value-added tax (VAT)
  • EU have additional tax when your customers are in EU
  • WooCommerce and Easy Digital Downloads integrate Taxamo to help users with confusing tax laws
  • Securing Your Site
  • Understanding the basics of Secure Sockets Layer (SSL)
  • You have to secure your page to ensure that your customers are safe from third parties from intercepting your customer's private information
  • You can use services like paypal to secure your customers’ purchases
  • You would need a SSL certificate to run HTTPS for secure websites
  • While accepting credit cards directly on your site is visually appealing, the commitment to PCI compliance is pretty demanding for a small business. It's much easier and safer to start with an off-site gateway.
  • Protect sensitive data
  • Even though you have SSL installed, you need to force your customers to use SSL and prevent them from using regular HTTP when they’re on a page with sensitive data
  • Processing even one transaction unsecurely could be devastating to your business.
  • Using WordPress for Ecommerce
  • Use native WordPress platforms
  • Try to use platforms build on top of WordPress
  • Wordpress will provide everything for you unless if you want to use amazon to process credit cards, then you’ll need to purchase the Amazon add-on
  • WooCommerce is good also, but updates are slow because they don’t want to break any pre-existing services
  • E commerce is incredibly complex and you need to make sure you're keeping your system up-to-date. In the last year, WooCommerce put out two dozen updates most of which are bug fixes.
  • This has a little to do with the quality of WooCommerce code and everything to do with the complexity of E Commerce.
  • Implement third-party platforms
  • It’s very easy to implement third-party platforms, but you won’t be able to edit them as you want
  • But some third-parties will take a higher transaction fee
  • You will little control over the product page
  • Some third-parties charge a monthly fee for the use of their services
  • Testing and Optimization
  • Test your site
  • Before you launch any website, you would want to test it first
  • Check the cart and checkout to make sure that it won’t cache too much
  • Make sure that the live shipping quotes are working correctly
  • Make sure you can get paid
  • Test gateways to check functionality to make sure you’re getting paid
  • Make sure discounts are adding up correctly
  • Test each service you’re connected to
  • Make sure to triple check everything on your site
  • Optimize your website
  • Install an analytic tool to make sure you know what’s happening on your website
  • Track your customers and see what they want from your website
  • Figure out if they get confused with your website
  • Optimizing your website can increase the amount that stay on your website and purchase products
  • After optimization make sure to test your tweaks
